The Master Key: COVENANT
A covenant is not a contract. A contract exchanges property; a covenant exchanges persons. It creates a family bond between parties not previously related. Two perfect examples: Marriage — a man and woman enter as strangers and leave as one family. Adoption — a child becomes truly yours through solemn oath. God has been making covenants — trying to bring us into his family — from the very first pages of Genesis. Every time humanity breaks it, God initiates a new one. This is the entire plot of the Bible.
👤
The Mediator
In ancient covenant-making, one person represented the group. That person is the mediator. Six great mediators stand out: Adam · Noah · Abraham · Moses · David · Jesus. Each mediator represents a widening circle — from one couple, to all humanity, to all creation. Subsequent mediators do not cancel previous ones — they fulfil and expand them, like concentric rings. Jesus is the outermost ring, encompassing all humanity for all time.
⛰️
The Mountain
Every major covenant is made on a mountaintop — Eden, Ararat, Moriah, Sinai, Zion, Calvary. Mountains are places of encounter with God where distractions fall away: Lonely — easier to focus on God. Elevated — God's-eye view. Things look smaller — less daunting. Everything in context. "Mountaintop experience" is not just a metaphor — it describes every decisive moment in salvation history. The Eucharist brings the mountaintop into every parish, every Sunday.

Why Does God Deal With Us Through Covenants?

"Because He wants to make us His family, though we are not so by nature."

The Eucharist is the New and Eternal Covenant — the fulfilment of all God's promises through Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, David, and the Prophets. At every Mass the covenant is renewed until he comes again.
